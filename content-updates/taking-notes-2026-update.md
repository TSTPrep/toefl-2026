## Understanding the New Audio Formats

Before learning note-taking techniques, understand what you're listening to:

### The 4 New Task Types

**1. Listen and Choose Response** (20-30 seconds)
- Very short conversational exchanges
- Select appropriate response
- **Note-taking**: Minimal‚Äîfocus on listening comprehension

**2. Conversation** (2-3 minutes)
- Campus conversations (student-professor, student-student, student-staff)
- Similar to old format conversations
- **Note-taking**: Moderate‚Äîtrack problem, solutions, key points

**3. Announcement** (30-60 seconds)
- Short campus announcements, instructions, notifications
- **Note-taking**: Light‚Äîcapture main point and 1-2 key details

**4. Academic Talk** (2-3 minutes)
- Lecture-style content (science, history, social studies)
- Similar to old format lectures but SHORTER
- **Note-taking**: Heavy‚Äîcapture structure, examples, relationships

### Key Difference from Old Format

**Old lectures**: 5-6 minutes (you had time to write detailed notes)
**New audio**: 30 seconds - 3 minutes (you must be selective!)

**Strategy shift**: Write less, listen more actively.

## Three Rules for Taking TOEFL Notes

These universal rules apply to ALL note-taking on the TOEFL, whether audio is 30 seconds or 3 minutes.

### Rule #1: Write Down Only Nouns, Verbs, and Adjectives

**Why this works**: These three parts of speech carry the meaning. Articles (a, an, the), prepositions (in, on, at), and pronouns (he, she, it) don't add much information.

**What changed in 2026**: This rule is MORE important because audio is shorter. You have less time to write, so every word must count.

### Examples by Task Type

**Listen and Choose Response** (20-30 seconds):
- Audio: "I'm having trouble accessing the online library database."
- Write: `trouble, access, library database`
- Skip: "I'm having," "the online"

**Announcement** (30-60 seconds):
- Audio: "The campus fitness center will close early on Friday at 5 PM for maintenance."
- Write: `fitness center, close, Friday 5PM, maintenance`
- Skip: "The," "will," "at," "for"

**Conversation** (2-3 minutes):
- Audio: "The professor suggested I visit the writing center before submitting my research paper."
- Write: `prof suggested, writing center, before submit, research paper`
- Skip: "The," "I," "my"

**Academic Talk** (2-3 minutes):
- Audio: "Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy through chlorophyll in plant cells."
- Write: `photosynthesis, converts light‚Üíchemical energy, chlorophyll, plant cells`
- Skip: "through," "in"

### Why It Works

**Test it**: Read your notes aloud with only nouns, verbs, and adjectives. You'll find you can reconstruct the meaning of the audio.

**Result**: 40-50% less writing, 100% of the meaning captured.

## Rule #2: Write Down Consonants, Not Vowels

**The technique**: Drop most vowels from words, keep only consonants.

**Why this works**: Your brain fills in the vowels when you read back your notes. Writing is 30-40% faster.

### Consonant Abbreviation Examples

| Full Word | Note Form | Meaning Clear? |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| professor | prfssr | ‚úÖ Yes |
| assignment | asgnmnt | ‚úÖ Yes |
| research | rsrch | ‚úÖ Yes |
| experiment | exprmnt | ‚úÖ Yes |
| photosynthesis | phtsntss | ‚úÖ Yes |
| psychology | psyclog | ‚úÖ Yes |
| economics | ecnmcs | ‚úÖ Yes |

### When to Keep Vowels

**Short words (3-4 letters)**: Keep vowels
- "exam" ‚Üí `exam` (not `xm`)
- "test" ‚Üí `test` (not `tst`)
- "due" ‚Üí `due` (not `d`)

**Reason**: Very short words are quick to write fully, and abbreviations can be ambiguous.

### Special Note for 2026 Short Audio

For **Listen and Choose Response** and **Announcements** (30-60 seconds), you might not even need consonant abbreviations‚Äîthe audio is so short that you can write full words if needed.

**Use consonant abbreviations for**:
- Conversations (2-3 minutes)
- Academic Talks (2-3 minutes)

## How to Take Notes for TOEFL Listening Conversations

Conversations in the 2026 format are **2-3 minutes** (similar to old format) and typically follow this structure:

### Conversation Structure

1. **Opening** (10-15 seconds): Context, who's talking
2. **Problem/Issue** (30-60 seconds): What's wrong or needed
3. **Discussion** (60-90 seconds): Possible solutions, details
4. **Conclusion** (10-20 seconds): Next steps, resolution

### Note-Taking Strategy

**Track these key elements**:
- **WHO**: Student (S) talking to Professor (P), Staff (St), or Another Student (S2)
- **WHAT**: The problem or question
- **WHY**: Context or reason for the problem
- **SOLUTIONS**: Options discussed (usually 2-3 options)
- **OUTCOME**: What the student will do

### Example Conversation Notes

**Audio** (2 minutes):
*Student talks to professor about missed assignment deadline due to illness. Professor suggests extension or alternative assignment. Student chooses extension.*

**Your notes**:
```
S ‚Üí P
? missed asgnmnt deadline
‚àµ sick last week
Solutions:
  1. extension ‚Üí need doctor note
  2. alternative asgnmnt
S chooses: extension, will get dr note
```

**Questions you can answer from these notes**:
- What is the student's problem?
- Why did the student miss the deadline?
- What solutions were discussed?
- What will the student do?

## How to Take Notes for TOEFL Listening Academic Talks

Academic Talks are **2-3 minutes** (much shorter than old 5-6 minute lectures!) and usually follow this structure:

### Academic Talk Structure

1. **Topic Introduction** (20-30 seconds): What the talk is about
2. **Main Concept/Process** (60-90 seconds): Key idea explained
3. **Examples/Details** (30-60 seconds): Supporting evidence
4. **Conclusion** (10-20 seconds): Significance or application

### Note-Taking Strategy

Use the **Cornell Method** adapted for short lectures:

**Left column**: Main topics (points 1, 2, 3)
**Right column**: Details, examples, relationships

### Example Academic Talk Notes

**Audio** (2.5 minutes):
*Biology lecture on photosynthesis: process, components, importance*

**Your notes**:
```
PHOTOSYNTHESIS

Main:
- light energy ‚Üí chemical energy
- happens in plants

How:
- chlorophyll absorbs light
- CO2 + H2O ‚Üí glucose + O2

Why matters:
- food for plants
- O2 for atmosphere
- ! base of food chain

Example:
- tree leaf: millions chloroplasts
- sunny day ‚Üí more photosynthesis
```

**Key difference from old format**: You don't have time to write detailed definitions or extensive examples. Focus on **structure** (main idea ‚Üí supporting points ‚Üí significance).

## Special Strategies for Short Audio (New in 2026!)

The 2026 format includes very short audio clips (20-60 seconds). These require different note-taking approaches:

### For Listen and Choose Response (20-30 seconds)

**Don't over-note**! The audio is so short that extensive notes interfere with comprehension.

**Strategy**:
1. **Listen first** (don't write anything)
2. **Jot 1-2 keywords** during the audio
3. **Answer immediately** after audio ends

**Example**:
- Audio: "Hey, did you finish the biology reading? I'm completely lost on the cell division chapter."
- Your note: `? bio reading, cell div`
- Answer: Select response showing you understood the question was about biology reading

### For Announcements (30-60 seconds)

**Track the 5 W's**: Who, What, When, Where, Why

**Strategy**:
- Write while listening (audio is longer than Response task)
- Focus on specific details (dates, times, locations)
- Use symbols for efficiency

**Example**:
- Audio: "Attention students: The campus bookstore will have extended hours this week Monday through Thursday from 8 AM to 8 PM for textbook buyback."
- Your notes: `bookstore, extended hrs, Mon-Thurs, 8AM-8PM, textbook buyback`

These 5-10 words capture everything you need to answer questions.

## Common Note-Taking Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

### Mistake #1: Writing Complete Sentences

**Why it's bad**: You fall behind and miss information while writing
**Fix**: Nouns, verbs, adjectives only + symbols

### Mistake #2: Writing Too Much for Short Audio

**Why it's bad**: Note-taking interferes with comprehension on 20-60 second clips
**Fix**: For short audio (< 1 minute), listen first, jot minimal notes

### Mistake #3: Not Using Abbreviations

**Why it's bad**: Writing full words is 40% slower
**Fix**: Consonants only + core symbols (practice until automatic)

### Mistake #4: Messy, Unorganized Notes

**Why it's bad**: Can't find information when answering questions
**Fix**: Use structure (bullet points, indents, columns) even for short notes

### Mistake #5: Trying to Remember Instead of Writing

**Why it's bad**: Human memory fails, especially under test pressure
**Fix**: Write it down! Even 5-10 keywords help tremendously
