### ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Update: TOEFL Changed on January 21, 2026**

If you're planning to take the TOEFL, you need to know about some major changes that went into effect on **January 21, 2026**. ETS (the company that makes the TOEFL) rolled out the biggest update to the test in over a decade.

**Here's what changed:**

**New Section Order**: The test now goes Reading ‚Üí Listening ‚Üí **Writing** ‚Üí Speaking (Writing moved before Speaking)

**Adaptive Testing**: The Reading and Listening sections now adjust difficulty based on how you perform. Think of it like a video game that gets harder or easier depending on your skill level. You'll complete Module 1, then Module 2 will adapt to match your ability.

**New Task Types**: All four sections have completely new question formats. For example:
- Reading now includes "Complete the Words" (filling in missing word halves)
- Speaking is just 2 tasks now (down from 4) - "Listen and Repeat" and "Take an Interview"
- Writing has 3 tasks including writing emails and building sentences

**New Scoring System**: Instead of the old 0-30 scale per section, you now get a 1-6 band score (in half-point increments like 4.5, 5.0, 5.5). This aligns better with international standards (CEFR levels).

**Good news?** The test is now approximately 85-90 minutes, which is significantly shorter than the old 120-minute format.

**Don't panic!** The information below still applies - you just need to know that the specific question types and strategies have evolved. We've updated all our articles and practice materials for the 2026 format, so you're in good hands.
