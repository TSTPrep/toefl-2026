### Speaking Tip #7: Master the Two New Task Formats

The 2026 TOEFL Speaking has just two tasks, and knowing what to expect is half the battle.

#### Task 1: Listen and Repeat (~7 sentences)

This task tests your **pronunciation, fluency, and pacing**. You'll hear a sentence, get 15 seconds to prepare, then 15 seconds to repeat it as accurately as possible.

**Strategy**:
- Focus on **exact repetition**, not paraphrasing
- Match the speaker's **rhythm and intonation**
- Don't rush - clarity beats speed
- If you mess up a word, keep going smoothly

**Practice approach**:
1. Listen to podcasts or YouTube videos
2. Pause after each sentence
3. Repeat it exactly as spoken
4. Record yourself and compare

**Pro tip**: The sentences get progressively harder. The first 2-3 are warm-ups (simple vocabulary, short length). Sentences 4-7 test complex grammar, longer phrases, and tricky pronunciation.

#### Task 2: Take an Interview (~4 questions)

This simulates a **real conversation** where you answer follow-up questions about a topic. You get **NO prep time** - just 45 seconds to respond to each question.

**Common interview topics**:
- Daily routines and preferences
- Educational experiences
- Work and career plans
- Hobbies and interests
- Problem-solving scenarios

**Sentence starters that work for ANY interview question**:

| Situation | Opening Phrase |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Giving an opinion** | "Personally, I think..." / "From my perspective..." / "To be honest..." |
| **Describing experience** | "In my experience..." / "I remember when..." / "One time..." |
| **Explaining preference** | "I prefer X because..." / "The reason I like X is..." / "X works better for me since..." |
| **Comparing options** | "On one hand... but on the other hand..." / "While X has benefits, Y is..." |
| **Elaborating** | "What I mean is..." / "In other words..." / "For example..." |
| **Handling uncertainty** | "I've never thought about that before, but..." / "That's an interesting question..." / "If I had to choose..." |

**The key difference from the old TOEFL**: This is conversational, not academic. Speak naturally like you're talking to a friend, not reading an essay.

**Practice approach**:
1. Have a friend ask you random questions
2. Answer immediately without planning
3. Speak for 45 seconds non-stop
4. Focus on sounding natural and conversational
